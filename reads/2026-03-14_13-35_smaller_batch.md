# Experiment Summary

## Hypothesis
Reducing TOTAL_BATCH_SIZE from 2^19 to 2^18 would allow more training steps within the 15-minute time budget, leading to better convergence.

## Change Made
Changed `TOTAL_BATCH_SIZE = 2**19` to `TOTAL_BATCH_SIZE = 2**18` in train.py. This halves the gradient accumulation steps from 64 to 32, approximately doubling the number of training steps.

## Result
- val_bpb: 0.528346 (baseline: 0.630820) - **16% improvement**
- training_seconds: 900.0
- peak_vram_mb: 1777.1 (same as baseline)
- mfu_percent: 10.48
- total_tokens_M: 37.5
- num_steps: 143 (baseline: 78) - **84% more steps**
- num_params_M: 50.3 (same model)
- train_batch_size: 4

## Expected?
Yes - with fewer gradient accumulation steps, each step is faster, allowing ~2x more training steps in the same time budget. More steps led to better convergence and lower val_bpb.

## Decision
- Keep
