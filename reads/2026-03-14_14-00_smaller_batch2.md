# Experiment Summary

## Hypothesis
Further reducing TOTAL_BATCH_SIZE from 2^18 to 2^17 would allow even more training steps within the 15-minute time budget, leading to better convergence.

## Change Made
Changed `TOTAL_BATCH_SIZE = 2**18` to `TOTAL_BATCH_SIZE = 2**17` in train.py. This reduces gradient accumulation steps from 32 to 16, approximately doubling the number of training steps again.

## Result
- val_bpb: 0.481693 (previous best: 0.528346) - **9% improvement**
- training_seconds: 900.4
- peak_vram_mb: 1777.1 (same as baseline)
- mfu_percent: 10.39
- total_tokens_M: 35.9
- num_steps: 274 (previous: 143) - **92% more steps**
- num_params_M: 50.3 (same model)
- train_batch_size: 4

## Expected?
Yes - halving the batch size again approximately doubled the steps, leading to better convergence and lower val_bpb.

## Decision
- Keep
